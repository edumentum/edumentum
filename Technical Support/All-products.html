                          New FL Research: Predict FAST scores with Exact Path

                          A 2024 study provides evidence that Exact Path diagnostic assessments yield highly accurate predictions of student test results on the Florida Assessment of Student Thinking (FAST). This research study linked fall and winter Exact Path diagnostic scores to FAST scores from the first two progress monitoring windows (PM1/fall and PM2/winter) in grades 6-8 in math and 6-10 in English language arts. Linking study results demonstrated that Exact Path Diagnostic scores could be used to predict whether students would be above or below the FAST passing cut score with 82%-91% accuracy.                           Student Time on Learning Path Monitoring

                          Empower students to take control of their academic growth. 

                          Our new student time on task tracker allows students to monitor the time they’ve spent during the week in each of their Exact Path learning paths. 

                          Students who complete 8+ skills a semester typically spend 40 minutes per week, per subject in Exact Path and experience proven growth on high-stakes assessments. This new student tool puts the outcomes of our research within their reach, allowing them to track progress towards this weekly goal and motivating them to be self-reliant learners as they pave their path to success. 

                          How does it work?

                          This new tool is available for all students K-12. Students select the clock icon from their menu to see 'This Week's Time on Task - Learning Path'. This is the total amount of time the student has spent actively working on learning path activities (lesson, practice, progress check). This does not include time spent on assignments, diagnostics, or while inactive (idle time).

                          Fluency Tasks to Enhance Universal Screening

                          We've added 2 new K-3 fluency task types to allow educators to identify and address students' decoding, word reading, and spelling difficulties: Nonsense Word Fluency and Letter Sound Fluency.

                          Nonsense Word Fluency tasks are used to screen for possible decoding problems and Letter Sound Fluency tasks are a strong predictor of reading readiness, such as word reading and spelling. These 24 brand-new oral fluency tasks can be used in conjunction with the reading diagnostic and other fluency tasks to universally screen students for reading difficulties including indicators of dyslexia.

                          Exact Path K-3 fluency tasks are one-minute oral fluency assessments designed to calculate words per minute and reading accuracy. Students read a printed passage aloud while the teacher simultaneously records any errors on their own printed document. These tasks can be regularly administered to inform instructional needs and results can be tracked within the system for progress reporting. When the Exact Path reading diagnostic is administered in combination with a fluency task, teachers leverage the resulting data to universally screen for reading difficulties and determine whether a student has risk factors for dyslexia.

                          new

                          On-Target Grade and Editable Target Dates

                          We introduced two pacing and progress-focused updates designed to help drive student accountability toward successful on-time course completion and success.

                          On-Target Grade offers educators and students a meaningful new data point for tracking performance, by providing an average grade of all completed and scored activities students are expected to have completed by the target date. While Current Grade provides an average of only work students have completed, and Course Grade provides an average of all work available in the course—both those activities students have and haven’t completed, On-Target Grade splits the difference, by answering the question: Based on my expectations of where students should be tracking, what is the learner’s grade right now?

                          Additionally, we've made On-Target Grade even more powerful by also introducing editable Target Dates. This update allows educators to make adjustments to when specific activities should be completed, either for an individual student or for entire sections. Do you want to give a specific student an extension on an activity? Edit the Target Date of when that activity is due, and the learner isn’t penalized for their tardy assignment in their On-Target Grade.
